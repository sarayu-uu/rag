Climate change refers to long-term changes in global temperatures and weather patterns. While some variations are natural, human activities have significantly accelerated climate change over the past century. The primary cause is the increase in greenhouse gases such as carbon dioxide and methane, mainly due to burning fossil fuels, deforestation, and industrial processes.

One of the most visible impacts of climate change is global warming. Rising temperatures are causing glaciers and polar ice caps to melt, leading to rising sea levels. This poses a threat to coastal communities and ecosystems. Extreme weather events such as hurricanes, heatwaves, droughts, and floods are becoming more frequent and severe.

Climate change also affects biodiversity. Many species struggle to adapt to changing temperatures and habitats, leading to extinction risks. Coral reefs, for example, are highly sensitive to temperature changes and are experiencing widespread bleaching.

Agriculture is another sector significantly impacted. Changes in rainfall patterns and temperature can reduce crop yields and affect food security. Farmers are forced to adapt by changing planting schedules or using different crop varieties.

Efforts to combat climate change include reducing greenhouse gas emissions, transitioning to renewable energy sources, and promoting sustainable practices. International agreements like the Paris Agreement aim to limit global temperature rise and encourage cooperation among countries.

Individuals can also contribute by reducing energy consumption, using public transportation, recycling, and supporting eco-friendly products. Small actions, when combined, can have a significant impact.

In conclusion, climate change is a global challenge that requires immediate and sustained action. While its effects are already being felt, proactive measures can help mitigate future damage and protect the planet for future generations.
